Fairfax Electrical Permits & Regulations

Understanding local permit requirements and electrical codes for your Fairfax project.

Fairfax City Electrical Permits

Fairfax City requires electrical permits for most electrical work, including panel replacements, new circuits, and major renovations. The Fairfax City permit office is located at City Hall on Armstrong Street, and permit applications can also be submitted online through the city's portal.

Common projects requiring permits in Fairfax City:

  • Electrical panel replacements or upgrades
  • EV charger installations (Level 2)
  • New circuit installations
  • Whole-house rewiring
  • Generator installations

As your local Fairfax electricians, we handle the entire permit process for you, ensuring all work is code-compliant and properly inspected.

Fairfax County Electrical Requirements

Fairfax County follows the Virginia Uniform Statewide Building Code (USBC) and the National Electrical Code (NEC) with some specific local amendments. The county's Land Development Services department oversees permits and inspections throughout Fairfax County.

Important Fairfax County electrical requirements:

  • AFCI protection requirements for bedrooms and living areas
  • GFCI protection for kitchens, bathrooms, and outdoor outlets
  • Specific EV charger installation regulations
  • Historic district special requirements (in designated areas)
  • Load calculation requirements for panel upgrades

Our team stays current with all Fairfax County electrical codes to ensure your project meets or exceeds all requirements.

Frequently Asked Electrical Questions in Fairfax

Common questions from Fairfax homeowners about electrical services

How much does it cost to replace an electrical panel in Fairfax?

The cost to replace an electrical panel in Fairfax typically ranges from $2,400 to $3,500, depending on the amperage and complexity. A standard 200-amp panel replacement in most Fairfax homes costs around $3,000. Older homes in areas like Old Town Fairfax may require additional work due to outdated wiring, potentially increasing costs. We provide detailed, transparent quotes after an on-site assessment of your specific situation.

Do I need a permit to install an EV charger in Fairfax?

Yes, both Fairfax City and Fairfax County require electrical permits for Level 2 EV charger installations. This is because these chargers typically require a new 240-volt circuit and potentially a panel upgrade. As licensed electricians in Fairfax, we handle the entire permit process for you, ensuring your EV charger installation is code-compliant and passes inspection. The permit requirements help ensure your charging system is installed safely and won't overload your home's electrical system.

How long does a typical electrical project take in Fairfax?

Project timelines in Fairfax vary by type: panel replacements typically take 1 day; recessed lighting installations 1-2 days depending on the number of fixtures; EV charger installations usually 4-6 hours. For Fairfax residents, permit processing can add 3-5 business days to the overall timeline. We coordinate closely with Fairfax City and County inspectors to minimize delays and can often schedule inspections within 24-48 hours of project completion.

Is my Federal Pacific panel in my Fairfax home dangerous?

Yes, Federal Pacific Electric (FPE) panels, commonly found in Fairfax homes built between 1950 and 1990, are considered dangerous and a fire hazard. The Consumer Product Safety Commission has identified these panels as failing to trip when they should during overloads or short circuits. We've replaced hundreds of these panels throughout Fairfax County, including many in the Kings Park, Mantua, and Fair Lakes neighborhoods where they were frequently installed. We recommend immediate replacement of FPE panels to protect your home and family.

What are common electrical issues in older Fairfax homes?

Older Fairfax homes, particularly in established neighborhoods like Mosby Woods, Country Club Hills, and Old Town Fairfax, commonly face issues with outdated wiring (knob-and-tube or aluminum), insufficient electrical panels (60 or 100 amp), lack of grounded outlets, and inadequate circuits for modern appliances. These homes often need panel upgrades, rewiring, and additional circuits to safely handle today's electrical demands. Our Fairfax electricians specialize in updating these historic homes while preserving their character and ensuring safety and code compliance.
